Shibuya Station




Shibuya Station Redevelopment



The Shibuya Station is an underground subway station that serves many people that are trying to get to work, but is also surrounded by an abundance commercial areas. It is one of Japans most dense districts, because of this, the Shibuya area has gone through and is going to go through some changes.


Tokyo Corporation, East Japan Railway Company and Tokyo Metro Co., Ltd, had proposed some plans to the Governor of Tokyo to undergo an urban planning project. This plan involves three areas above and around Shibuya station, these areas being the Shibuya Station Area, Shibuya Station South Area, and Shibuya Dogenzaka Area. The primary focus of this plan is to: “1) form a network for pedestrians that is comfortable and easy to understand by strengthening the function of transportation nodes; 2) adopt an urban function that increases the appeal of the city as the genesis of lifestyle and culture and elevates its international competitiveness; and 3) strengthen the area’s disaster-preventing function and improve the environment”.





Shibuya Station Area



The Shibuya station area is located directly above Shibuya Station. This area is the going to be the primary attraction to the area. A 46-storey building (approximately 230 meters high), is going to be built on the proposed area. The plan is to have offices on the higher levels of the building and commercial facilities on the middle and lower floors. Pedestrian walkways and plazas will also be integrated into the development of this area.


By implementing these plans, the congestion of foot traffic will be greatly reduced, therefore increasing the safety of pedestrians when using Shibuya Station. Furthermore, by creating commercial and office buildings, it is thought that more domestic and overseas corporations will go to Shibuya, thus increasing the growth of creative content industries and increasing the number of visitors to Shibuya.



Shibuya Station South Area





The Shibuya Station South Area is planned to have a 33-storey building (approximately 180 meters high). This building will be multifunctional as office building will be located at the higher levels while the middle and lower levels will be used for creative content industries. The area will also be filled with pedestrian pathways, underground pathways and green walkways. All of this is to be located next to the Shibuya river, adding to the attractiveness of the area.


The Shibuya Station South area will host a new entrance into the underground subway station. By doing this it alleviates traffic amongst other entrances, as well as creating traffic and visitability to this new area of Shibuya. The content creative industries in this area is planned to bring in more industries from around the world, hence leading to the growth of the industry within Japan.


Conculsion



To assess the success of the proposed urban transformation plan of the Shibuya Station Areas data will need to be collected. The proposed plan states that they plan to bring in more visitors and both domestic and international industries to Shibuya, data which could help see if this is successful would be: the number of tourists to visit the area (prior vs new), the number of industries to have moved into the area (prior vs new), and possible the economy of the district (prior vs new). The plan also implies that it wants to decrease the congestion of foot traffic in the area, data that could be useful in determining the success of this could be: the density/congestion of the entrances into the station (prior vs new), or the density/congestion of walkways in general (prior vs new). Another aspect that the plan touched on was the safety of pedestrians in terms of the congestion of traffic and in the case of a disaster, data that could determine if the plan was successful in this regard could be the number of injuries that was caused due to congested walkways/entrances (prior vs new), the number percentage of people evacuated in the case of a disaster(prior vs new), or traffic accidents caused buy congested crossings (prior vs new).
